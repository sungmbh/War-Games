📋 Program Overview

The CES War Games simulates real-world CES deal-making. 20 startup teams compete to win the most money by pitching to 7 real buyer personas through 3 qualification gates.

🎯 Core Concept

  • Real Sales Funnel: Research → Auction → Pitch → Qualify → Negotiate
  • Real Stakes: Teams invest fake money to book meetings, earn it back based on performance
  • Real Pressure: Time limits, competition, and authentic buyer evaluation
  • Real Learning: Experience actual CES dynamics in a safe environment

🎮 Game Mechanics Summary

GATE 1: PITCH

Duration: 5 minutes

Entry Fee: US$2k–US$5k bid

Payout: US$3k if interested

Goal: Get buyer interested in your startup

GATE 2: QUALIFY

Duration: 12 minutes

Entry Fee: US$2k

Payout: US$8k / US$5k / US$0

Goal: Prove you're ready with evidence

GATE 3: NEGOTIATE

Duration: 15 minutes

Entry Fee: US$3k

Payout: US$15k / US$10k / US$0

Goal: Structure a real deal

🎯 Buyer Decision Guide

Keep it simple! Buyers should focus on authentic evaluation, not complex scoring.

GATE 1 AUCTION - Who to Accept?

✅ ACCEPT IF:
  • Startup is relevant to what you buy
  • Email is personalized (mentions you specifically)
  • Good bid amount (US$3k+)
  • Professional communication
❌ DECLINE IF:
  • Wrong fit for your focus area
  • Generic template email
  • Low bid + weak personalization
  • Unprofessional approach

Tie-breaker Rule

If 2 startups are equally relevant, higher bid wins. This shows confidence and seriousness.

Example: Two healthcare startups email Johnny (Healthcare CVC). Both relevant. One bids US$2,500, one bids US$4,000. → Accept the US$4,000 bid.

GATE 2 & 3 - Simple Tiers

GATE 2

Strong (US$8k): Proven, impressive, ready

Decent (US$5k): Good potential, needs work

Weak (US$0): Not convinced, too early

GATE 3

Deal (US$15k): Would commit, strong fit, ready to go

Conditional (US$10k): Interested but concerns remain

No Deal (US$0): Not ready or wrong fit

After each decision, text: "Team [Name] - Gate [1/2/3] - US$[Amount]"

Example: "Team CardioMirror - Gate 1 - US$3k"

⚠️ Critical Risk Mitigation

PROBLEM: Buyer Text Delay

Risk: Buyers forget to text Kyle after decisions

Impact: Leaderboard becomes inaccurate

Solution:

  • Volker reminds buyers during breaks
  • Kyle can ask buyers directly
  • Update retroactively if needed
  • Cheat sheets remind buyers to text
PROBLEM: Lost Cash

Risk: Team loses or miscounts money

Impact: Score disputes

Solution:

  • Team's responsibility (stated upfront)
  • No reimbursement (game design feature)
  • Kyle's tracker is final authority
  • Spot-check top 3 at final count
PROBLEM: Auction Chaos

Risk: Too many emails, buyers overwhelmed

Impact: Slow responses, team frustration

Solution:

  • 30-minute auction window
  • Buyers check every 5 minutes
  • Accept best 2–3 only
  • Clear decision criteria
