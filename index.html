Red Flags & Green Flags
Quick pattern recognition

üö´ Red Flags

  • No paying customers (only pilots or LOIs)
  • Weak unit economics (burning cash, unclear pricing)
  • Vague transaction ("we want to partner" with no specifics)
  • Team unable to answer basic financial questions
  • No clear differentiation from competitors
  • Unrealistic timelines or market assumptions

‚úÖ Green Flags

  • Multiple paying customers with retention data
  • Clear CAC < LTV with path to profitability
  • Specific transaction with realistic terms
  • Team demonstrates deep domain expertise
  • Defensible IP or strong network effects
  • Proven traction in a large, growing market

Tough Questions to Ask
Use these to probe deeper in Gates 2 & 3

üí∞ Traction & Revenue

  • Who are your paying customers by name?
  • What's your MRR/ARR and growth rate?
  • What's your customer retention rate?
  • How did you acquire your first 10 customers?

üìä Unit Economics

  • What's your CAC and how do you calculate it?
  • What's your LTV and payback period?
  • What's your gross margin?
  • When will you be cash flow positive?

üéØ Strategy & Competition

  • Who else is solving this problem?
  • Why would a customer choose you vs. alternatives?
  • What's your defensible moat?
  • What happens if [big company] enters your space?

ü§ù Deal Specifics

  • What if I counter with [different terms]?
  • What happens if [risk scenario] occurs?
  • Who else are you talking to?
  • What's your walk-away point?
