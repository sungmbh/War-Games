Program Overview

Goal: Finish with the most cash by 4:00 PM. Money is the single scoreboard. Auction pressure in the morning; diligence and negotiation in the afternoon.

Scarcity: 18 teams advance from Gate-1. ≤7 total Gate-3 negotiations (max one per mentor).
Phase What Happens
Research (10:00) Build CES target list (20 people + one-sentence “why” each). Optional War Room prep (US$1,000).
Gate-1 Auction → Discovery Bid US$2,000–US$5,000 (1k steps) for a 5-min meeting. Payout: US$3,000 if “Interested,” else US$0. 18 total meetings.
Gate-2 Qualification 12-min Q&A (no pitch). Fee: US$2,000. Payouts: US$8,000 / US$5,000 / US$0 (Strong / Decent / Weak). Capacity: 18 total across mentors (≤3 each).
Gate-3 Negotiation 15-min deal discussion. Fee: US$3,000. Payouts (same regardless of instrument): US$15,000 / US$10,000 / US$0 (Deal / Conditional / No Deal). ≤7 total.
Intel Station Open 2:00–3:45. 8-min buyer consults. Payouts: US$0 / US$1,000 / US$2,000; mentor sets value; mentor tracks.
Hot Lead (2:30) 5-minute blitz. Winner earns US$5,000 bonus + a meeting slot (recovery path for morning “outs”).

Flows by Role

Startup Flow
  1. Research 20 targets (+ one-sentence “why”).
  2. Optional War Room prep (US$1k).
  3. Auction email to your best-fit buyer (cc: ops address). If rejected, immediately email backup buyer(s) until slots fill.
  4. If accepted, pay Jade your bid → Gate-1 5-min meeting → Earn US$3k (Interested) or US$0.
  5. Gate-2 with same buyer if scheduled (12-min Q&A; pay US$2k; earn US$8k/5k/0).
  6. Use Intel Station and Hot Lead to recover or stack cash.
  7. Gate-3 (pay US$3k; earn US$15k/10k/0).
  8. Final Count at 16:00.
Mentor Flow
  1. Review 2–3 lists per wave at 10:45 → award US$0/1k/2k.
  2. During Auction (11:30–12:00) accept up to your Gate-1 cap via quick replies; if “over-popular,” follow router rule (auto-redirects overflow by ops).
  3. Gate-1: 5-min meetings → text Kyle “Team – Gate 1 – Interested/Pass – 3000/0”.
  4. Gate-2: up to 3 slots (12-min, no pitch) → text Kyle “Strong/Decent/Weak – 8000/5000/0”.
  5. Intel Station: post “Available” and price US$0/1k/2k → self-track.
  6. Gate-3: ≤1 slot per mentor (Deal/Conditional/No Deal) → text Kyle “15000/10000/0”.
MC (Volker)
  • Open, explain rules, call waves to the second.
  • Auction cadence: 11:30, 11:35, 11:40, 11:45, 11:50, 11:55, 12:00.
  • CP1 12:35, CP2 14:00, Final 16:00.
  • Hot Lead at 14:30 (drama + 5-min timer).
Ops (Jade & Kyle)
  • Router (manual): Ops reassign overflow to under-subscribed mentors in real time.
  • Jade: collect bids/fees; lockbox; fast check-in.
  • Kyle: live leaderboard; slot board; receive standardized texts.

Booking, Time & Slot Math

Gate Duration Capacity Booking Logic
Gate-1 5 min 18 total meetings Mentors accept via email; Jade takes team bid; Kyle logs “Interested/Pass.” Two teams get no meeting (email quality/fit/latency).

Manual router: No software needed—Ops maintains a whiteboard of remaining mentor capacity and redirects overflow accordingly.

Intel Station vs Ken’s War Room

Intel Station
  • Walk-up 8-min consults with any available mentor.
  • Mentor sets value per session: Earn 0 / 1 / 2.
  • Great for recovery (morning outs) and stacking cash.
  • Mentor self-tracks; reported at checkpoints.
War Room (Ken)
  • Paid strategy deep-dives (10–12 min typical).
  • Pay 1 in morning; 1–2 in afternoon.
  • Focus: target selection, emails, bid sizing, Gate-3 deal framing.
  • Ken reports totals to Kyle at checkpoints.
